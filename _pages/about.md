I am currently pursuing a Ph.D. at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), specializing in Advanced Multimodal Interaction Models and Robotic Agents for Large-Scale Orchestration of Complex Tasks. My academic journey and professional experiences have consistently revolved around the intersection of artificial intelligence, robotics, and human-computer interaction.

My research primarily focuses on developing sophisticated multimodal interaction models that enable more intuitive and effective communication between humans and robotic agents. These models are designed to integrate various forms of input, such as speech, gestures, and visual cues, to create a more natural and seamless interaction experience. By leveraging advancements in machine learning and computer vision, my goal is to enhance the capability of robotic systems to understand and respond to complex human instructions in real-time.

A significant aspect of my research involves the orchestration of complex tasks in large-scale environments. This entails creating frameworks and algorithms that allow multiple robotic agents to collaborate efficiently, ensuring that tasks are completed with a high degree of precision and coordination. The potential applications of this research are vast, ranging from industrial automation and logistics to healthcare and disaster response.

Before joining MIT, I completed my Master's degree in Mechatronics and Robotics at New York University, where I worked on projects involving autonomous navigation and machine learning. My undergraduate studies were in Computer Sciences at the National University of Mexico, where I developed a strong foundation in robotics and control systems.

In addition to my academic pursuits, I have had the opportunity to collaborate with industry leaders on various projects. These collaborations have provided me with valuable insights into the practical challenges and opportunities associated with deploying advanced robotic systems in real-world scenarios. My work has been published in several peer-reviewed journals and conferences, and I have presented my findings at international symposiums.

I am deeply committed to advancing the field of robotics and AI through both theoretical research and practical applications. My vision is to contribute to the development of intelligent systems that can seamlessly integrate into various aspects of human life, enhancing productivity and improving quality of life. I am also passionate about mentoring and inspiring the next generation of engineers and researchers, and I actively participate in outreach programs aimed at promoting [STEM education](https://stemedresearch.siu.edu/what-is-stem-education/).
